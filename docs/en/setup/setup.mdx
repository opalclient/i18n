<SetupCard icon="settings" title="Setup Instructions" description="Step-by-step guide to getting started with Opal.">
<Steps>

<Step number={1} title="Create Your Account">
Register on the [dashboard](/dash) and purchase or activate your license. Once activated, set up your integrations:

- **Discord** — connect your Discord account under Settings → Connections for community features
- **Spotify** — connect Spotify for in-game music integration (requires creating a Spotify app — follow the instructions on the connections page)
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Download the Launcher">
Head to the [Downloads](/dash) page on your dashboard and download the **Opal Launcher** for your platform (Windows, macOS, or Linux).

<Note>The launcher handles Fabric installation, mod management, and automatic updates for you.</Note>
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Configure the Launcher">
Open the launcher and sign in with your Opal account. From here you can:

- **Settings** — configure your Minecraft installation path, Java version, and memory allocation
- **Mods** — manage your mod list alongside Opal (Fabric API is required)
- **Packs** — install and manage mod packs
</Step>

<Step number={4} title="Launch & Enjoy">
Press **Launch** and you're in. The launcher handles authentication, updates, and loading automatically. You'll be prompted to sign in with your Opal account on first launch.
</Step>

</Steps>
</SetupCard>

<SetupCard icon="settings" title="External Launcher Setup" description="For users who prefer Prism Launcher or another external launcher.">
<Steps>

<Step number={1} title="Install Fabric">
Download and install [Fabric Loader](https://fabricmc.net/) for Minecraft 1.21.10 using your preferred launcher.
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Download Opal">
Download the Opal Loader from your [dashboard](/dash) and place it in your mods folder alongside [Fabric API](https://modrinth.com/mod/fabric-api/versions?g=1.21.10).
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="JVM Arguments">
Add the following JVM arguments to your launch profile:

<JvmArgs>
{`-XX:+DisableAttachMechanism
--add-opens java.base/java.nio.file.spi=ALL-UNNAMED
--add-opens java.base/java.lang=ALL-UNNAMED
--add-opens java.base/java.lang.reflect=ALL-UNNAMED
--add-opens java.base/jdk.internal.loader=ALL-UNNAMED
--add-opens java.base/sun.security.ssl=ALL-UNNAMED
--add-opens java.management/sun.management=ALL-UNNAMED`}
</JvmArgs>
</Step>

<Step number={4} title="Launch">
Launch Minecraft with your Fabric profile. You will be prompted to sign in with your Opal account.
</Step>

</Steps>

<Note>If using an external launcher, you are responsible for managing Fabric, mods, and JVM arguments yourself. The Opal Launcher handles all of this automatically.</Note>
</SetupCard>

<SetupCard icon="alert" title="Common Errors" description="Known error codes and their solutions.">
<ErrorList>
<ErrorItem code="Launch Error (No Code)" title="Missing Java Arguments">
The required JVM arguments are missing from your launcher profile. This only applies to external launchers — the Opal Launcher sets these automatically.
</ErrorItem>

<ErrorItem code="0x5e" title="Incompatible Launcher">
The JVM arguments are missing or your launcher doesn't support them. Switch to the Opal Launcher or a supported external launcher like Prism.
</ErrorItem>

<ErrorItem code="4e-63 / 4f-63" title="Connection Blocked">
The client loader can't download Opal. This is common for users in China or Russia where raw TCP traffic is blocked. Fix: connect through a **VLESS proxy** with a stealth protocol. Other users may see this with a weak or unstable internet connection — try a different network.
</ErrorItem>

<ErrorItem code="G-26" title="HWID Mismatch">
Your hardware ID doesn't match. Reset your HWID on the [dashboard](/dash) under account settings (available every 30 days), or open a support ticket for an early reset.
</ErrorItem>

<ErrorItem code="0x31" title="Debugging Software Detected">
A program like HTTP Debugger or similar traffic inspection tool is installed or running. Close or uninstall it and try again.
</ErrorItem>

<ErrorItem code="No Error" title="Game Launches but Opal Doesn't Load">
Two common causes:

**Missing Fabric Loader** — you have the Fabric API mod but not the Fabric Loader itself. You need both. The Opal Launcher handles this automatically.

**Disconnected from socket** — type `.online` in chat. If the command auto-completes but doesn't return a player list, you're disconnected from the server. Check your internet connection or try a VPN.
</ErrorItem>

<ErrorItem code="Launch Error" title="Stored Credentials Issue">
If you see a launch error with no code, your stored credentials may be corrupted. Delete credentials containing "wtf.opalxterminus" and "wtf.opal" from:

- **Windows:** Credential Manager (Control Panel → Credential Manager → Windows Credentials)
- **macOS:** Keychain Access (delete entries containing "wtf.opal")
- **Linux:** Delete from your keyring (gnome-keyring or equivalent)
</ErrorItem>
</ErrorList>
</SetupCard>

<SetupCard icon="alert" title="Linux-Specific Issues" description="Additional troubleshooting for Linux users.">
<ErrorList>
<ErrorItem code="Chat Spam" title="No Dialog Service">
If you see repeated spam in chat logs or nothing appears after Minecraft-type log output, you need a dialog popup service installed. Search for your distro's equivalent (e.g., zenity, kdialog).
</ErrorItem>

<ErrorItem code="Crash (wtf.opal.*)" title="Missing Keyring">
If the client crashes with an error referencing `wtf.opal`, install and start a keyring service:

- Install `gnome-keyring` (or your desktop's equivalent)
- Ensure the keyring service is running before launching
</ErrorItem>
</ErrorList>
</SetupCard>

<SetupCard icon="faq" title="FAQ" description="Frequently asked questions.">
<FAQList>
<FAQItem question="How can I use Opal on older version servers?">
By using [ViaFabricPlus](https://modrinth.com/mod/viafabricplus). This lets you connect to 1.8 and other older version servers while running Minecraft 1.21.10.
</FAQItem>

<FAQItem question="Do I need the Opal Launcher?">
No, but it's strongly recommended. The launcher handles Fabric installation, JVM arguments, mod management, and automatic updates. External launchers require manual setup.
</FAQItem>

<FAQItem question="I have a lot of mods and something is crashing. How do I find the problem?">
Use a binary search: remove half your mods, test again. If it still crashes, remove half of the remaining. If it works, swap back and repeat. Keep Fabric API and Opal in place — they're required. This narrows down the conflicting mod quickly.
</FAQItem>

<FAQItem question="I'm in China/Russia and can't connect.">
Internet censorship in some regions blocks the raw TCP traffic Opal uses. Connect through a VLESS proxy with a stealth protocol to fix this. See the purchase page for more details.
</FAQItem>

<FAQItem question="How do I reset my HWID?">
Go to your dashboard → Settings → Account. HWID resets are available once every 30 days. If you need an early reset, open a support ticket on Discord.
</FAQItem>
</FAQList>
